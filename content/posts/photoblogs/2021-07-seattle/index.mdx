#### The first, and likely the last, trip where I shot digital.

Film was developed & scanned at [Lago Vista Film Lab](https://www.lagovistafilmlab.com/).

On July 4th a couple weeks prior I won an ebay bidding brawl for a [Nikon
FM2n](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikon_FM2), and this was the first trip I
busted it out.

I was eager to try it - digital photos started to feel a bit "sterile" to me.
But, that's probably because I found myself just slapping an "S" tone curve on
whatever I shot, paralyzed by the idea that everything needed more contrast.

While I don't think it looks bad... I was longing for something else. I enjoy
photography as a way to relive memories, and as such I find myself shooting at
the backs of friends while they're walking, random scenes, and (often shitty)
candids. I felt like film's color profiles, and shooting process (you get <36
shots instead of thousands) would highlight that style more.

Unfotunately, it's a lot more expensive, and takes _longer_ to process and edit (if
scanning yourself). But, those are other topics.

I liked the results from this trip so much I ended up buying an M6 a couple
months later, and my a7iii is now essentially a webcam + film scanner.

**Side note**: My inspiration to descend into this beautiful money pit of a passtime
was/is from [GrainyDays](http://www.jasonkummerfeldt.com/). He's straight money.
